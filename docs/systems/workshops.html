System Workshops

Looking to start or reinvigorate a design system? We’ll workshop with your group to teach individuals and align teams pursuing design systems.

What We Cover

During one or two days together, we’ll combine lecture and hands-on activities. If the standard, out-of-the-box agenda suits your needs, excellent! Otherwise, we’ll tailor topics and hands-on activities to your needs.

For public venues and organizations looking for learning fundamentals, we’ll follow a standard agenda that hits all the important topics.

  • 8:30AM - 9:00AM

    Arrival & Setup

  • 9:00AM - 10:45AM

    Segment 1: Planning a System

    • Define your design system, including relationships across systems
    • Explore your library’s scope of visual style, UI components and more
    • Plan a path from nothing (or a previous version) to launch a new system
    • Identify, prioritize and prepare products that’ll adopt the system

  • 11:00AM – 12:30pm

    Segment 2: Starting a System

    • Create a new visual language, from conceptual reference designs to a Sketch sticker sheet
    • Discover a UI component inventory by deconstructing existing product experiences
    • Organize your library into topics, sections, and assets everyone understands

  • 12:30pm – 1:30pm

    Lunch (provided by you)

  • 1:30pm – 3:00pm

    Segment 3: Making the System

    • Form a predictable process for designing, building, and documenting a library
    • Deliver on promises of quality and scale via a rigorous system QA practice
    • Establish an information architecture strategy for documenting design and code assets
    • Design effective component doc, from conventional displays to custom guidance

  • 3:30pm – 5:00pm

    Segment 4: Practicing Systems

    • Form a shared vocabulary for your system’s roles: team members, users, and contributors
    • Identify the regular activities and processes to operate a systems team
    • Strategize on how to coordinate with and support adopting products
    • Build an inclusive and participatory community of contributors and experts

Most in-house teams choose to customize their agenda to tailor topics to their current needs. This may result in a workshop lengthened to two days or shortened a half day (usually paired with a sponsored talk for a larger group).

Example 1: Half Day for Organization with Multiple Systems

In one instance, a company paired an sponsored talk to the local community with a customized half-day in-house workshop. They chose to focus on aligning multiple in-house system efforts around a future strategy along topics covering how to practice systems. As a result, they omitted topics of system design, code and documentation.

Prepping for the Event

Once we sign an agreement, we’ll meet with you at least three times over one to two weeks to plan the event.

Meeting 1 (60 min)

Draft Agenda & Logistics

During our first call, we'll review available topics and activities, discuss who should attend why, and talk space & timing.

Meeting 2 (90 min)

System & Product Tour

During a second call, we'll immerse ourselves in the existing design, code and docs of your system(s) and tour the products it targets.

Meeting 3 (60 min)

Finalize Agenda

We'll review a proposed agenda and any customized materials and finalize logistics.

Who Should You Invite?

The workshop format is suitable for up to 100 participants and covers content relevant to many disciplines beyond just designers and engineers.

Core Participants

System workshops work best with:

  • Assigned staff already making system(s)
  • UX and visual designers
  • Front-end engineers
  • Design and engineering leaders

Optional Participants

Expand the workshop to include:

  • Product managers
  • Project managers
  • Content strategists
  • Researchers
  • Executive sponsors

Why a Workshop with EightShapes?

With EightShapes’ seasoned experience and enthusiasm, you’ll inspire your teams and spark systems work. Your team can use a workshop to:

  • Kickoff or energize in-house systems efforts
  • Define your system and what it offers
  • Frame how to form a system team and energize a community
  • Plan how to create and adopt a system
  • Refine how to design and code features like color, buttons, and space
  • Develop a shared vocabulary for dispersed teams
  • Achieve outcomes and teach via with engaging hands-on activities
