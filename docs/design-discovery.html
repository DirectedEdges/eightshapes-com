Design Discovery

Even the most efficient product teams must take a moment to ask themselves, “But are we designing the right thing?“

Discovery answers this question, but it’s not as easy as that.

What You Get

Discovery begins the design process for something new or for improving what you already have. Discovery’s outcome supports a crucial decision: how should we move forward? Our aim is to make the next steps as clear and direct as possible, therefore our discovery projects yield a prototype and a plan.

Concept Prototype (the “What”)

Capturing the overall design direction, the concept prototype shows the crucial parts of the user experience.

Design Plan (the “How”)

Establishing the design direction and how to execute it.

Getting to Green Light

Discovery concludes with a crucial inflection, the decision about moving forward. Your team’s green light may require aligning around a direction, getting executive buy-in, securing funding, or all of the above. We will work with you to determine the criteria for a green light, and to produce a prototype that meets those criteria.

What We Do

Our discovery engagements include two phases. In the first phase we gather and process information about your design problem. In the second, we explore possibilities and focus on a solution.

Phase 1: Assess & Define

Articulate the problem, constraints, and user needs.

Often includes: User research, Competitive audit, Content review, Prioritization workshop, Information architecture, Application mapping, Vision and requirements

Phase 2: Prototype & Plan

Establish the design direction and how to execute it.

Often includes: Design workshop, Design reviews, Initial prototype, Testing concepts, Creating a plan

Typical Discovery Engagement

Discovery entails four kinds of activities: gathering information relevant to the design problem; processing information to put the data into context; exploring possible design directions; focusing solution ideas into an executable plan. We can configure these activities to suit your schedule and budget.

Transitioning to Detailed Design

Discovery gives us the vision and the plan. Detailed design gives us the specifics. With your team bought into the concept, we switch to an iterative approach for fleshing out the details.
